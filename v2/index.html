Anjouan Gaming

The regulatory authority for internet gaming on the Autonomous Island of Anjouan.

Regulatory Mandate

Anjouan Gaming administers the licensing, supervision, and enforcement of internet gaming operations under the laws of the Autonomous Island of Anjouan. The authority operates as the designated regulatory body of the Anjouan Offshore Financial Authority and the Anjouan Gaming Board.

All internet gaming operators within the jurisdiction are required to hold a valid licence and comply with defined regulatory standards. The authority maintains oversight of licensed operators through ongoing supervision, compliance monitoring, and enforcement action where conditions are breached.

Key Functions

Regulatory Framework

The authority establishes and maintains the regulatory standards, compliance requirements, and legal conditions under which internet gaming licences are issued and administered.

Licensing

Applications for B2C and B2B internet gaming licences are assessed against defined due diligence, technical, and financial criteria. Licences are issued subject to conditions and ongoing obligations.

Enforcement

The authority monitors compliance and takes enforcement action where licence conditions are breached. Suspended and revoked licences are published in the public record.

Public Register

A publicly accessible register of all licensed operators, including licence status, is maintained by the authority. Any party may verify the validity and standing of an issued licence.

Jurisdiction

The Autonomous Island of Anjouan is a constituent island of the Union of the Comoros. Internet gaming licensing is administered under Anjouan law through the Anjouan Offshore Financial Authority.

The regulatory framework incorporates provisions addressing anti-money laundering, counter-terrorist financing, responsible gaming, and fair play standards. Licensed operators are subject to ongoing supervision and periodic compliance review.
